# Job Search

Methodologies for career transitions, applications, and offer negotiation. Treat a job search as a funnel: many inputs, few outputs — optimize the ratios, not the volume.

## The Application Funnel

A job search has measurable stages. Track the conversion rate at each one rather than counting raw applications.

1. **Applications submitted** — the top of the funnel.
2. **Recruiter screens** — first signal that the résumé is landing.
3. **Hiring-manager / technical rounds** — middle of the funnel.
4. **Final / onsite rounds** — late-stage.
5. **Offers received** — the outcome.

Healthy baseline: 20–40 targeted applications → 8–12 recruiter screens → 3–5 onsites → 1–2 offers. If a stage's conversion ratio drops below ~25%, the problem is upstream of that stage: the résumé, the referral strategy, or the interview performance that precedes it.

## T-Shaped Skills for Career Transitions

Hiring managers pattern-match on depth. A candidate switching fields without demonstrated depth looks like a dabbler. Build a T:

- **The vertical bar** — one domain where you can speak in specifics. Shipped projects, measurable outcomes, hard trade-offs you've lived through.
- **The horizontal bar** — literacy across adjacent areas. Enough to collaborate, ask sharp questions, and not be blindsided.

For a career pivot, plan at least 8–12 weeks of concentrated depth work (portfolio, proofs-of-work, writeups) before applying.

## Time Phasing a Career Transition

Weeks 1–4: **foundation** — update résumé, portfolio/writeups, LinkedIn. Map target roles to required skills. Identify 3–5 concrete gaps.

Weeks 5–10: **proof-of-work** — ship one substantive project per gap. Write it up in public. Reach out to 1–2 warm contacts per week.

Weeks 11–14: **search activation** — apply with referrals preferred. Track applications in a spreadsheet with status + last-touch date. Aim for 10 high-quality applications per week, not 50 spray-and-pray.

Weeks 15+: **interviewing** — practice at least 4× per week if interviews are active. Do company-specific prep, not generic leetcode marathons.

## Compensation Negotiation

- Never give a number first. "I'm flexible; I'd like to understand the full range for the role."
- Anchor on market data (Levels.fyi, Blind, salary bands).
- Negotiate the full package: base, equity, sign-on, relocation, start date, PTO.
- Silence is a tool. After they state a number, wait.

## Clarification questions a goal-clarifier should ask for job-search goals

- What specific role title(s) are you targeting, at what seniority?
- Current state: employed, unemployed, planning to leave in N weeks?
- What's your hard deadline (visa, savings runway, life event)?
- Which of these is weakest: résumé, referrals, interview performance, portfolio?
- Are you geographically constrained or open to remote?
- Target comp range, and is it negotiable for the right role?

## Common failure modes

- **Spray-and-pray applications** — high volume, low conversion; indicates the résumé or targeting is the bottleneck, not effort.
- **Perpetual prep** — 6 months of "getting ready" without applying. Set a date for the first 10 applications and hold it.
- **Ignoring referrals** — cold applications convert ~2%. Referred applications convert ~30%. Spend time on the 30% path.
- **Accepting the first offer without leverage** — even if you accept it, negotiate. The marginal value per hour of negotiation is usually the highest of the year.
